\section{Recommendation}

I built the team's recommended JEPA (first a minimal GRU-JEPA, then a proper \textbf{masked,
action-conditioned TS-JEPA}) \textbf{and} the simplest peer (\texttt{x(t)}-as-latent,
monotone-by-construction) as an honest benchmark. Both are \textbf{constraint-valid} (zero violations),
non-collapsing, and auditable, engaging the brief's ``accurate, on-manifold, \textit{and} auditable''
bar (zero-violation $\neq$ \textit{on-manifold}; separated in Sec.~5). \textbf{My recommendation is TS-JEPA
for the Digital Liver world-model, made by measurement, not assertion.}

On the \textit{deliberately clean, fully-observed} toy a well-constrained baseline edges JEPA on raw
point-accuracy (ratchet MAE \textbf{0.033} vs \textbf{0.039}, K=24, free rollout): a simpler model is
right \textit{if the data ever looked like this}. But the real domain is defined by three stresses this
toy strips out. On the one that dominates real sensor data (noise), \textbf{JEPA wins decisively,
outside seed noise}; on the other two it draws level or edges ahead (baseline vs TS-JEPA, ratchet MAE;
multi-seed gated; \texttt{figures/}, seed-0):

The pattern is not luck: as the data gets more realistic (noisy, stale, unseen), JEPA moves from
\textit{behind} on the toy to \textit{level-or-ahead}, decisively so under noise. The baseline wins only
the sanitised slice, and even that win is \textit{partly borrowed}: it hardcodes generator structure we
won't have in production, and being memoryless and low-dim it cannot scale to the real modalities (MRI,
histology, 3-D shape). \textbf{So, concretely: the coupled baseline is the delivered prototype for the
clean 8-D exercise} (\texttt{baseline.pt}, best point-accuracy, the constraint showcase); \textbf{TS-JEPA
is the architecture I recommend for the real, noisy, sparse, high-dim Digital Liver}, measured winning,
not merely proposed.

\section{What a predictive latent buys, and precisely when it pays}

Predicting a future \textit{representation} rather than raw values pays when: (1) observations carry a
large \textbf{stochastic/nuisance substrate} (imaging speckle, sensor noise) a raw loss would waste
capacity chasing (JEPA's headline advantage); (2) the dynamics' natural coordinates \textbf{are not the
raw channels}; (3) there is \textbf{hidden state} to infer and carry; or (4) raw targets are
high-dimensional/multimodal. The brief points squarely at (1): the real pipeline ``has its own stochastic
substrate on top of \texttt{x(t)},'' stripped out on the clean slice. \textbf{So I put the conditions back
and measured} (Sec.~1 scorecard, Sec.~6): each stress JEPA is built to exploit, noise (denoising), staleness
(history-integration), unseen speeds (abstraction), is one where it draws level or wins, noise
decisively. \textbf{The clean slice is the \textit{one} place JEPA doesn't pay; restore the domain's real
stresses and it does.}

\section{Did the JEPA objective cost accuracy? I measured it, and found my own bug}

The brief calls this tension the heart of the exercise; I chased it by measurement, and the honest arc
matters more than a clean story.

\textbf{First attempt, and a wrong conclusion.} My minimal GRU-JEPA measured a $\sim$10$\times$ gap (0.52
vs 0.05) and I nearly wrote it up as a \textit{fundamental} auditability-vs-expressiveness tension. It
wasn't; it was a \textbf{decoder/target-space mismatch}: the decoder saw only the \textit{predicted}
\texttt{zhat}, never the invariance \textit{target} \texttt{enc(x\_\{t+1\})}, so the better the latent
prediction, the more \texttt{zhat} landed where the decoder couldn't read it. \textbf{The fix (one term):}
decode the true future embedding through the \textit{same} head $\to$ \textbf{0.52 $\to$ 0.12}, latent
prediction still real (\texttt{inv/var} $<$ 0.1). Lesson (D14): when a \textit{trivial} problem looks like
a fundamental limit, suspect your own compute graph first.

\textbf{Then the team's actual architecture} (diagram: \texttt{figures/arch\_tsjepa.png}; the shared
by-construction head: \texttt{figures/arch\_constraint\_head.png})\textbf{.} A masked, action-conditioned
\textbf{TS-JEPA}: over a transformer feature$\times$time grid, mask the future \textit{state} but
\textbf{keep the known treatment plan} (UDCA/ERCP tokens, we know the plan, not the outcome); predict
the EMA target-encoder's embeddings at masked months; decode \textbf{by construction} (cumsum of
non-negative increments from the last observed state; S = creep $-$ ERCP relief; fast fields via sigmoid;
four-term VICReg+dec-anchor loss). Five seeds: \textbf{0.039 $\pm$ 0.006, zero violations}, a step
behind the baseline's 0.033 on the clean slice.

\textbf{Verdict, measured.} The masked TS-JEPA is accurate, on-manifold (same cumsum-from-observed-state
construction as the baseline, Sec.~5), and auditable; \textbf{on the clean slice it \textit{ties}} the
constrained baseline (simpler and more stable), \textbf{and on the domain's real stresses it \textit{wins}
(Sec.~1, Sec.~6).} \textbf{Why only a tie when clean?} The state is near-deterministic and low-rank (intrinsic
effective rank \textbf{2.83}), and its one hidden driver is a static scalar the state already reflects:
a GRU recovers it ($R^2$=0.66), yet an \textbf{oracle} given the true value gives no gain. Clean, the
latent has almost nothing to abstract; re-introduce the stochastic substrate or partial observation and it
does. That is why JEPA is the pick for the real problem.

\textbf{The three named peers.} \textit{\texttt{x(t)}-as-latent} is the benchmark peer (it wins the clean
slice). \textit{A plain Neural-ODE} buys continuous-time sampling regular monthly data doesn't need and
still needs the same head (D0). \textit{A supervised decoder} is the baseline's family; the value is the
\textit{constrained} parameterisation.

\section{Representation collapse: the learned-latent risk, engaged}

A learned latent can collapse to a constant the predictor trivially matches. \textbf{Mechanism:} VICReg
variance + covariance terms plus a reconstruction anchor (\texttt{z\_t $\to$ x\_t}); an early version
omitting the anchor collapsed (D6). \textbf{Metric:} effective rank (\texttt{exp}-Shannon-entropy of the
covariance eigenspectrum) vs the data's \textbf{intrinsic} dim (2.83, same estimator), not the nominal 16:
with VICReg it holds $\sim$2.1, without it \textbf{collapses to $\sim$1.3}; TS-JEPA adds a BYOL-style EMA
target. Necessary and effective, but, per Sec.~3, not \textit{sufficient} to make the latent worth its
cost on the clean slice.

\section{Constraints on-manifold, and what they cost}

The one-directional fields are enforced \textbf{by construction}: F, D, P, M as \texttt{prev + softplus($\cdot$)}
clamped to bounds (a decrease is \textit{unrepresentable}; a smooth clamp-free variant measured
\textit{worse}, D23), A/C/flare as bounded \texttt{sigmoid($\cdot$)}, S as a monotone creep minus an
ERCP-gated relief (resolving a spec contradiction on S's direction, D1). Result: \textbf{exactly zero
violations} across every model and rollout, a property of the parameterisation, not the loss. The model
\textit{uses} the exception: at ERCP it predicts the S step-down \textbf{149/149} times ($\Delta$S $-0.173$
vs true $-0.174$) while creeping up otherwise (\texttt{probe\_metrics.py}). The alternatives the brief
names I rejected: a \textit{loss penalty} (only discourages) and \textit{projection} (guarantee lives
\textit{outside} the model).

\textbf{But zero violations $\neq$ on-manifold.} A learned manifold critic (\texttt{manifold\_critic.py},
AUC 1.0 on valid-but-wrong transitions) scores the baseline \textbf{0.996} and \textbf{TS-JEPA 0.963}
(single-seed) against \textbf{real} genuine generator transitions \textbf{0.995} (both on-manifold),
but the naive \textbf{GRU-JEPA 0.726} (off-manifold \textit{despite} 0 violations: step-by-step
re-encoding compounds error). The cumsum-from-anchor construction both share stays on the surface.
Constraints bound the box; the critic checks you are on it.

\textbf{Engaging the coupling (the interesting part).} Per-field monotonicity is easy; I built the hardest
coupling into the head, \textbf{M as a hazard of sustained F$\cdot$C}: M's increment is \textit{gated by
prev F$\cdot$C} (with C=0, M can't move, \texttt{test\_invariants.py}). It holds (\texttt{corr($\Delta$M,
F$\cdot$C)} 0.57 $\to$ \textbf{0.98}), \textit{halves} M's error (0.053 $\to$ 0.028), and lifts the whole
model (0.037 $\to$ \textbf{0.033}); cirrhosis = g(F) is the same ``derive, don't predict'' readout. The
couplings I left \textit{learned} (flares$\leftrightarrow$A/C, treatment$\to$A/C) are soft/bidirectional
and resist a clean form, the honest remaining edge.

\section{An evaluation that could have falsified the model}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Accuracy vs the noise floor, not a flatline.} Error is reported beside the irreducible floor
(spread of independent generator re-runs): fast channels A/C/flare sit \textit{at} the floor; only
ratchets have reducible error, and there the model beats persist-last \textbf{$\sim$3$\times$} (0.033 vs
0.096) and predict-mean \textbf{$\sim$4.6$\times$} (\texttt{probe\_metrics.py}).
\item \textbf{Generalisation probe (out-of-distribution).} Baseline ratchet MAE at K=24 (in-dist
\textbf{0.033}): held-out susceptibility $\to$ \textbf{0.099 (3$\times$)}; unseen late treatment $\to$
\textbf{0.031} (unchanged, generalises over the \textit{observed} lever); beyond horizon $\to$
\textbf{0.100 (3$\times$)}. So: generalises over the observable, fails over the hidden/unseen. TS-JEPA
\textbf{slightly edges the baseline on held-out susceptibility} (a hint the latent helps on unseen
progression speeds) and ties on treatment, but its learned absolute positions \textbf{cap it at the
trained horizon}, a split, the recurrent baseline more capable there (\texttt{ts\_jepa.py}).
\item \textbf{Domain-stress probes, where JEPA earns its keep} (\texttt{missing\_visits.py},
\texttt{jepa\_denoise.py}; Sec.~1 scorecard, \texttt{figures/}). The clean probe ranks the baseline first; the
\textit{domain} probes flip it. \textbf{Sensor noise} (mechanism: \texttt{figures/arch\_denoised\_anchor.png})\textbf{:}
a noise-augmented JEPA forecasts from a \textbf{\textit{denoised} current-state anchor (a learned clean
estimate from the whole window, not the raw noisy observation)}, beating the baseline at every $\sigma$
(\textbf{$-37\%$ at $\sigma$=0.10}, 3 seeds; Sec.~1 table); an ablation reverting to the raw noisy anchor
collapses the gain to the baseline, proving the denoised anchor \textit{is} the mechanism, one a
memoryless model cannot have. It trades ``passes exactly through the observed value'' (undesirable under
noise anyway) while keeping monotone/bounded by construction. \textbf{Stale visit:} JEPA overtakes at
\textbf{$\sim$12--15 months} (3 seeds; Sec.~1). Each needs training \textit{for} the condition, the right
way to build for a noisy pipeline; K0$<$8 is outside the trained mask range.
\item \textbf{Clinical, decision-grade readouts} (\texttt{clinical\_metrics.py}). Cirrhosis risk
\textit{ranking} is strong (\textbf{AUC 0.927}; F\_pred 0.6--0.8 $\to$ 63\% truly cirrhotic), but
thresholded detection exposes a real failure: \textbf{decompensation recall 0.27} (median +14 mo late),
\textbf{cirrhosis-onset 0/20}, the point estimate under-shoots the tail: what aggregate MAE hid, and the
argument for Sec.~8's distributional head.
\item \textbf{The ceiling, plainly.} Within one generator, generalising = recovering the update rule, so
the probe ranks models and shows \textit{failure} but \textbf{cannot} settle ``world model vs.\
generator-inverter''. That needs a second generator, not built.
\end{itemize}

\section{``Why did the model predict decompensation at month 30?''}

Using portal hypertension P crossing a threshold as the decompensation proxy
(\texttt{figures/explain\_decompensation.png}):

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Structural, auditable for free.} \texttt{P(t) = P(t$_0$) + $\Sigma$ non-negative increments}
is a running total, every step inspectable, and \textbf{the same audit runs on the JEPA} (identical
accumulation): auditability transfers to the latent model.
\item \textbf{Attribution reveals a correlational shortcut} (model-faithful perturbation,
\texttt{explain.py}): the model keys the P-increment on \textbf{flare ($\sim$62\%)}, slow drivers F/P only
$\sim$16\%; flare \textit{leads} the A/C surges that raise the ratchets, so it's a faithful predictor
whose reasons are \textit{correlational, not causal}: the risk the brief flags.
\end{itemize}

\section{Residual risk and what I would do next}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Probabilistic forecasting (the biggest gap).} Sec.~6's tail miss is \textbf{aleatoric}
(susceptibility unidentified from short history), so a deep ensemble can't fix it (covers \textbf{28\%},
\texttt{ensemble\_forecast.py}). A \textbf{mixture-density head} (\texttt{mdn\_forecast.py}) lifts
\textbf{tail recall to 0.82} (cirrhosis @q90; the point estimate catches almost none of the tail, Sec.~6)
at no accuracy cost; a \textbf{persistent-latent CVAE} (\texttt{latent\_forecast.py}, D25, \textit{built,
not future work}) stabilises its calibration. The tail-aware \textbf{union} (persistent z + mixture-NLL)
lifts recall to \textbf{0.97} but is high-recall / low-precision, \textbf{experimental tail-risk
evidence, \textit{not} a calibrated readout} (D23--D27).
\item \textbf{Discrete latents (VQ-JEPA): a research direction, not a guaranteed win.} A VQ codebook
would commit each patient to one \textit{auditable archetype} and can't average the tail, but
discreteness alone doesn't fix a weak decoder (mine under-leveraged z, D27) and trades KL-annealing for
codebook-collapse. Named, not built (D28).
\item \textbf{Validate JEPA where it pays, partly done.} I put the domain's stresses back and JEPA won
(Sec.~1/Sec.~6). Next: the \textit{full} modality substrate (MRI/histology/3-D shape) the baseline cannot
parameterise at all, and a learned \textit{adaptive} anchor (denoise when noisy, trust the reading when
clean); the ablation shows both modes already live in one model.
\item \textbf{Causal, not correlational, reasons.} Mask information flow to the causal edges and validate
counterfactuals against generator re-runs, attacking the Sec.~7 shortcut.
\end{itemize}

\noindent\textbf{Bottom line:} I engaged JEPA for real (a minimal GRU-JEPA and the masked TS-JEPA),
made both constraint-valid, on-manifold (critic-measured; the naive GRU drifts, Sec.~5), and auditable, then
\textit{measured} what it buys: on the clean slice it ties a simpler constrained baseline; restore the
domain's real stresses and it \textbf{wins}: denoising noise ($-37\%$, ablation-proven), stale/missing
visits, unseen speeds. \textbf{The coupled baseline is the delivered prototype for clean 8-D forecasting;
TS-JEPA is the recommendation for the real noisy/sparse/high-dim pipeline.} My earlier ``fundamental
cost'' claim was my own bug, found, fixed, and reported, not buried.
